NSS Unit 8
IIT Kharagpur

National Service Scheme (NSS) is a voluntary organisation launched in Gandhiji’s birth century year, 1969. The programme aims to promote students’ personality development by the means of community service. Coming from humble beginnings, NSS initially had a meagre 37 universities under it, which has grown substantially over the years, now having more than 3.2 million student volunteers from all over the nation.

You can find out more about NSS here
About NSS IIT Kharagpur

In IIT-KGP, NSS has been made a part of core academic curriculum for close to one-third of the first and second year undergraduate students. The programme comprises of weekly activities held in villages and a minimum of one compulsory annual camp. Some of the activities NSS IIT-KGP indulges in include-
Regular activities-

  • Education- Children are the future of the country and an educated mind can do wonders for the society as well as the world. From each unit, a teaching team is selected who give weekly tutuions to village school children or children of poor workers in the campus.
  • Cleanliness and Environment preservation- Nature birthed us not to destroy it, rather to become a part of it. All volunteers contribute in tree plantation, garbage picking, purification of water resourses, ’tripal’ distribution for rain water conservation and waste management through indegenious techniques.
  • Spreading Awareness- We believe in spreading awareness to the common person, and what better way to do it other than something that catches their interest? For instance, we spread general awareness on issues of health and hygiene by organising rallies, street plays or ‘nukkad’ etc.
  • Not cash but ‘Kind’ donation- We as students cannot provide a constant monetary help to the people who truly need it, but that doesn’t mean it must stop us. Distribution of clothing and apt books to village residents is undertaken on a regular basis by the volunteers.
